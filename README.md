Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder, and its early diagnosis is highly challenging due to its subtle and gradual development. Traditional diagnostic methods rely heavily on clinical assessments, often leading to detection at later stages when treatment options are limited. With advancements in deep learning, convolutional neural networks have shown strong potential in improving early detection using brain imaging data. In this context, the **MobileNetV2 architecture** is utilized for its lightweight and efficient design, enabling accurate classification of early-stage Alzheimer's disease. Its balance between performance and computational efficiency makes it suitable for real-world medical applications, supporting timely and reliable diagnosis while also offering scope for further improvements with larger datasets and practical deployment systems.
